# Visual Style Guide

## Overall mood
The site should feel:
- dark
- neon
- expressive
- curated
- feminine edge
- polished but not corporate
- art-first, not template-first

## Color direction
Base:
- black
- deep charcoal
- near-black navy

Accent glow:
- cyan
- magenta
- purple
- electric blue

Support tones:
- muted gray
- soft lavender
- deep plum accents where needed

## What to avoid
- flat white-heavy layouts
- generic startup/web app styling
- cluttered thumbnails
- too many tiny cards
- too much text on first view
- boxed-in sections with no breathing room
- giant product dump energy

## Typography feel
Headlines:
- bold
- clean
- high contrast
- stylish but readable

Body text:
- simple
- modern
- easy to scan
- not too small

## Layout feel
- more breathing room
- bigger visuals
- cleaner hierarchy
- fewer things competing at once
- sections should feel intentional, not crowded

## Image treatment
- larger featured imagery
- fewer cards at once
- stronger crop consistency
- no random filler graphics
- portfolio images should feel like artwork, not thumbnails in a dump

## Homepage behavior
- hero should hit emotionally fast
- feature sections should be curated
- merch should preview, not overwhelm
- custom work should feel personal and clear
- contact should feel easy and inviting

## AO visual identity
AO should feel like:
- neon mood
- digital identity
- expressive personality
- creative confidence
- dark glam edge
- clean polish with attitude
